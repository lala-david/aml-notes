**Built a 22,000-line open-source AML textbook for virtual assets (Korean-first, English edition in progress)**

After months of work, I'm sharing an open, comprehensive AML study resource
for virtual asset compliance practitioners — with a strong focus on Korea
but globally comparative.

AML Notes → https://github.com/lala-david/aml-notes (link in first comment)

**Why**

Entry-level AMLOs, compliance analysts, and legal counsel in Korea have
consistently reported a gap:

- CAMS is US-centric
- Korean textbooks are regulation summaries
- There's no single bridge between statute, supervision, and tooling

**What's inside**

- 60-day daily curriculum — onboarding standard for entry-level AMLOs
- 29 topical notes — Korea, US, EU, APAC regulatory comparisons
- 1,200+ line supervisory inspection response workbook (4-week timeline)
- Technical rigor — Exposure Score formula, CIOH pseudocode,
  IVMS101 Travel Rule validator
- Current as of 2026-04 — Bybit USD 1.46B lessons, FATF R.16 (2025-06),
  Korea SFTIA 2026-01 major shareholder review
